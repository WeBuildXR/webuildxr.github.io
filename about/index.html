About WeBuildXR

Idea

The original idea was to build a collaborative "world building" experience, using WebXR and open platforms and protocols.

As a starting point, we decided to go with Babylon.js with ecsy with the stretch goal of adding WebRTC for collaboration and voice.

We also wanted a robust way of persisting and sharing the created worlds, so we settled for the industry standard "IFC" format, an open standard that is common in the Architectural, Design and Engineering industries.

Design

Since the theme of the hackathon was retro gaming, we wanted to add a distinct retro look and feel to the application. During testing, it turned out that low resolution textures and simple lighting indeed gives the experience a "retro" look all in itself.

Due to time constraints, we didn't get the WebRTC portions in the solution, but this is something that can hopefully be added afterwards.

As a nice side deliverable from this hackathon, we also created a starter project for anyone wishing to use web-ifc, a fast, open source IFC loader, in Babylon.js. The repo for the starter project can be found at https://github.com/anders-lundgren/web-ifc-babylon
